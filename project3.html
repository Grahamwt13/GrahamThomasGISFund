Problem

The Flint water crisis exposed residents to unsafe drinking water. This project identified areas of Flint at greatest risk by examining proximity to industrial pollution, rivers, and lead service lines.

Narrative

This project evaluated drinking water risk in Flint, Michigan by identifying locations where multiple environmental hazards overlap. Buffer analysis was used to identify areas near Toxic Release Inventory facilities, rivers, and lead service lines. Overlay analysis combined these criteria to classify properties according to risk level. The analysis revealed several clusters where multiple hazards intersect, highlighting neighborhoods that may require additional infrastructure investment and public health intervention.

GIS Tools and Skills Used

  • Buffer Analysis
  • Overlay Analysis
  • Spatial Join
  • Intersect
  • Attribute Queries
  • Risk Classification
  • Cartographic Design
